1.Idli


Idli, a staple of South Indian cuisine, is a beloved dish renowned for its soft, fluffy texture and mild, tangy flavor. Made from fermented rice and lentil batter, idlis are steamed to perfection in special molds, resulting in a healthy, low-calorie option ideal for breakfast or snacks. Often accompanied by sambar, coconut chutney, or other condiments, idli's versatility and nutritious profile have made it a popular choice not only in India but also worldwide, appreciated for its simplicity, taste, and ease of digestion.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup parboiled rice
  • 1/2 cup whole Urad Dal
  • 1/4 cup Fenugreek seeds
  • salt
  • water needed

Recipe

  1. Prepare a batter by soaking rice and lentils, grinding them into a smooth consistency, and allowing it to ferment.
  2. Season the batter with mustard seeds, cumin seeds, chopped green chilies, and curry leaves.
  3. Grease idli molds and pour the batter into them.
  4. Steam the idlis until cooked through.
  5. Serve warm with accompaniments like sambar and coconut chutney
